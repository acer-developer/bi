## Sector Intelligence — Key Facts for Sales Team

### 1. Bank Guarantees Are Critical
Construction companies require credit ratings to obtain Bank Guarantees (BGs) for project bidding and performance bonds. An INC or lapsed rating = **no BG = no project bid participation**. This is the highest-urgency pitch trigger for this sector.

### 2. Real Estate Developers Need Ratings for NCD Issuances
Many housing developers raise money through Non-Convertible Debentures. SEBI requires a credit rating. Developers who are INC or overdue face legal constraints on fundraising.

### 3. Tile/Stone/Granite Cluster (257 companies)
A large cluster of building material companies — granites, tiles, ceramics — primarily concentrated in Gujarat, Rajasthan, and Tamil Nadu. These are mid-market manufacturers with bank working capital needs. High INC rate suggests the rating quality they get doesn't match lender expectations.

### 4. EPC/Contractor Cycle Dependency
EPC contractors get rated at project inception, then let ratings lapse during project execution. They return to market for the next project cycle. Seasonally, this peaks before monsoon (April–June bidding season) and post-monsoon (October–December for new contracts).
