## Key Intelligence Findings

1. **Infrastructure is ACER's largest sector by volume (1,246 companies) and Infomerics is deeply vulnerable there (41.5% INC rate).** Every Infomerics infra INC company is a lead for ACER — 124 companies that have walked out on their rater and still need ratings for bank lines.

2. **12 BFSI companies are ULTRA HOT: both HIGH urgency (overdue) AND INC.** Infomerics holds 8 of these 12 — same pattern as Infrastructure. Infomerics appears to have the highest client dissatisfaction across all sectors, making their client book ACER's primary acquisition target.

3. **June is BELOW-AVERAGE for new issuances (7.7% of annual vs 9.7% in July).** This is actually a sales opportunity — companies approaching July renewals are currently negotiating mandates. ACER's window to capture pre-renewal mandates is NOW (June).

4. **Peak season is July and September.** ACER's sales team should front-load outreach in May-June to capture August-September mandate confirmations. Hiring / BD capacity should peak in Q2 (July-September) each year.

5. **The 160 doubly-INC companies cluster in Infrastructure (15%) and Agro/Food (9%).** Infrastructure over-representation suggests infra companies are more likely to engage multiple agencies AND more likely to stop cooperating — possibly due to project-linked rating disputes.

6. **52.5% of doubly-INC companies are "Other/Unknown" sector** — their company names don't contain sector keywords. These 84 companies need manual sector tagging or MCA enrichment to unlock sub-sector intelligence.
